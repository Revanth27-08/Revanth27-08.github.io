I am a Graduate student working with Prof. Dhiman Ray in the Department of Chemistry and Biochemistry, University of Oregon.  My research focuses on developing a mechanistic understanding of complex biological processes using high-level atomistic simulations.
My research aims to achieve a mechanistic understanding of complex biological processes such as the RNA conformational dynamics and drug–target interactions through atomistic simulations. These processes often occur on timescales that are challenging to access using classical Molecular Dynamics (MD) simulations. To overcome this limitation, I focus on accelerating these rare events using the enhanced sampling techniques. My work integrates statistical mechanics and machine learning approaches to explore free energy landscapes and transition kinetics in molecular systems.
